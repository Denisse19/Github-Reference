How to Create a Github Live Website

Step One - Create Repository

On the website you'll see the plus sign on the top right corner of the page, click on it and click on the first option,"New repository"

Step Two - Respository name

Give your repository a name. Make sure to make it public. IMPORTANT: Make sure you check mark the "README." than click on the green button "create repository."

Step 3 - Main Page

This is the main page you see once you create your repository. Here you can upload files and change the settings thats necessary for your website.

Step 4 - Uploading Files

This is where you upload your files, index.html and other folders you want to show up in your website. You can keep adding files to your website, you can add description on the chages you do.

Step 5 - Settings

Go on settings, and sroll to the bottom where it says "GitHub pages." On source, click down on "None" and click "master branch." It will take you back to the top.

Step 6 -

Scroll back down again to "GitHub Pages." There you will see your site link. If its blue its getting published, so wait a few minutes. Once it turns green its ready.
